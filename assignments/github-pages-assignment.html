New Terms

A new term for me is Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP), it's an open source HTML framework tailored for mobile web browsing, improving load and render times on mobile devices. Browsing the web on mobile is different from using a computer because mobile devices don't have the same resources, such as RAM, that computers do, hence why AMP was created.

Another new term for me is "Plug-ins" within the context of a CMS. Plug-ins are usually additional functionality that you can add to your website via downloading it and installing it in your CMS. They are usually made by third-party developers, thus you don't have to develop them yourself. They can improve your website either visually or functionally with minimal effort,

Full-Stack Development Process

Generally, there are three steps to the full-stack development process. The first is feature development, either your boss or product manager will come to the developers with a new feature to build. Once coded, it goes through a workflow to verify the changes won't cause problems, including unit testing, security checks, dependency checks, formatting, and other checks. The second step is QA, your changes are deployed to a dev or QA environment, where a QA engineer will attempt to break the feature and identify issues for you to fix. The third step is production, once your code passes QA, it gets pushed to the production servers where real users interact with it, and feedback is gathered, that can be through analytics, surveys, or other forms. Then the cycle repeats.
